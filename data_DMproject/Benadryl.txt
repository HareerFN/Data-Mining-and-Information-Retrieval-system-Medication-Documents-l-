4-Benadryl:
General Description:
Benadryl is a brand name for the antihistamine medication diphenhydramine. It is commonly used to relieve symptoms associated with allergies, such as sneezing, runny nose, itching, and watery eyes. Benadryl is available in various forms, including oral tablets, liquid solutions, and topical creams.

Side Effects;
1. Drowsiness and sedation: One of the most common side effects of Benadryl is drowsiness. It can cause sleepiness and impair mental alertness
2. Dry mouth and throat
3. Urinary retention: In some cases, Benadryl can cause difficulty in urination or urinary retention, especially in individuals with prostate enlargement or bladder problems.
4. Dizziness and coordination problems.
Prescription Requirement:
In many countries, Benadryl is available over the counter without a prescription. However, higher strengths or specific formulations may require a prescription.
Preparation and Usage:
1. Dosage: 
For Adults:
recommended oral dose for adults is 25-50 mg of Benadryl every 4-6 hours as needed, not exceeding 300 mg in 24 hours.
For Children:
-For children aged 6-11 years: The typical oral dose is 12.5-25 mg of Benadryl every 4-6 hours as needed, not exceeding 150 mg in 24 hours.
-For children aged 2-5 years: The recommended oral dose is 6.25-12.5 mg of Benadryl every 4-6 hours as needed, not exceeding 37.5 mg in 24 hours.


2. Administration: Benadryl is typically taken orally in tablet or liquid form. It can also be applied topically as a cream or lotion for certain skin conditions.
3. Avoid combining with other medications ; sedatives, tranquilizers.
Indications:
1. Allergic reactions.
2. Itchy skin conditions.
3. Sleep aid.
