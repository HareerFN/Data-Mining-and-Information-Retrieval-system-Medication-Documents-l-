Panadol Night is a medication used for the relief of pain and to aid sleep. It contains paracetamol and diphenhydramine hydrochloride as its active ingredients. Commonly used for conditions such as headache, toothache, muscular aches, period pain, and fever, Panadol Night comes in tablet form and is available without a prescription. Side effects may include drowsiness, dizziness, blurred vision, dry mouth, and constipation. The recommended dosage for adults and children over 16 years is 2 tablets at bedtime, while individuals with hypersensitivity to the ingredients or a history of such reactions should avoid it. It is essential to read packaging or consult a healthcare professional for accurate information, including potential interactions with other medications or medical conditions.
