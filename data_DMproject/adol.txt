Adol Syrup is a paracetamol-based medication used to relieve pain and reduce high temperatures. It is commonly used for teething pain, reducing fever, and relieving discomfort associated with infections such as cold and flu. Adol Syrup comes in liquid form and does not require a prescription. Common side effects may include nausea, vomiting, constipation, lightheadedness, dizziness, or drowsiness. The recommended dosage varies based on age, with specific guidelines for children between 6 and 12 years old, the typical dosage is 10-20ml of Adol Syrup, which can be taken up to four times daily. Children between 1 and 6 years old usually take 5-10ml, again up to four times daily. Infants between 3 and 12 months old typically receive a lower dosage of 2.5-5ml, up to four times daily.. It is important to note that Adol Syrup is contraindicated in cases of active liver disease or severe hepatic impairment, and caution should be exercised in individuals with mild hepatic impairment. It is advisable to consult the product packaging or seek advice from a healthcare professional for comprehensive and accurate information about Adol Syrup.
